Tomori the Penguin – The Internet’s Abstract Sensation

Internet figures By Weilin Published: 2026-04-18 00:00
In the chaotic and wonderful world of internet memes, few recent phenomena have achieved the viral longevity of ”Tomori the Penguin” — a bizarre yet beloved mashup of a shy anime singer and a waddling, screaming Antarctic bird. Born from the unholy marriage of BanG Dream! It‘s MyGO!!!!!’s protagonist and a nonsensical “baby scream,” this pixelated penguin has taken over group chats, fueled thousands of AI videos, and even crashed a real-life zoo. As of 2026, she remains one of the internet‘s most indestructible inside jokes.

Who Is Tomori — the Human?

Originally, there was Takamatsu Tomori, the soft-spoken, emotionally fragile 155 cm lead vocalist of the fictional band MyGO!!!!! [14†L19-L20]. Voiced by Yōmiya Hina and debuting in the 2023 anime BanG Dream! It‘s MyGO!!!!!, Tomori is a 155 cm tall, slow-moving “bizarre child” who obsessively collects small, round stones [2†L9-L10]. She also owns penguin-patterned band-aids and once took her bandmate Anon to an aquarium’s “penguin skywalk” — a scene so heartfelt that it became the fandom‘s emotional foundation [5†L4-L6]. For dedicated fans, penguins were always part of her aesthetic [9†L5-L6].

The 2025 Viral Explosion

The meme, however, didn‘t truly take flight until January 21, 2025, when Bilibili creator TuShanBaiBaiMiao uploaded a 20-second video titled gugugaga [10†L8-L9]. The clip, which has since amassed over 9 million views, spliced footage of Tomori‘s emotional expressions with a distorted ”gugugaga” — a screeching, baby-like sound originally stemming from an angry Roblox streamer [11†L5-L6] [9†L8-L9]. The audio was originally the sound of a Roblox player“s character being killed, rendered in deep, angry shouts of ”Gugu-GAGA!” [8†L7] [12†L9-L10]. The result was so absurd and perfectly timed that it immediately spawned thousands of derivative images: Tomori‘s head photoshopped onto the body of a penguin, often alongside the caption “I‘m from Antarctica, I love holding stones, inspiration mushroom scratch scratch, gugu and gaga” [10†L13-L14].

The Deification of “Aisazai”

By mid-2025, someone had 3D-modeled the “Tomori Penguin” as a wobbling, emoting creature, and the spread — at first confined to Bilibili and WeChat — was unstoppable. Fans began incorporating her into abstract dramas: a penguin screaming at a phone (“Why aren‘t you answering me? Is your phone broken?”), a penguin delivering motivational speeches, and a penguin dueting with fellow meme figure ”Manager“ (a penguin-like character from the game Arknights: Endfield) [12†L4-L6]. The Chinese internet had long embraced absurdist, ”brain rot“ humor; Tomori was its perfect vessel.

Tomorin the Real-Life Penguin

In a twist that felt almost too perfect, the meme jumped into reality. In late 2024, Taiwan’s Xpark Aquarium held an online poll to name a newborn king penguin chick [15†L12-L13]. MyGO!!!!! fans, noticing the opening, flooded the nominations with ”Tomorin” — Tomori‘s Japanese nickname. After facing allegations of ballot-stuffing, the aquarium not only validated the win but officially contacted the Japanese rights holders for approval [15†L17-L19]. The winning name received over 13,000 votes and was eventually announced as the official name of the baby penguin [6†L11-L12]. Even the character’s voice actress, Yōmiya Hina, later expressed a desire to travel to Taiwan to see the real-life “Tomorin” in person, calling the name ”incredibly cute“ [20†L14-L15].

The Abstract Fall

The penguin became a tool for ”chouxing“ (abstract) humor, stripped of all character depth. The sweet, insecure girl who wrote ”human songs“ became a shouting, waddling, stone-holding idiot penguin. But rather than harming the brand, the absurdity fueled an even stronger grassroots fandom. The meme crossed over into mainstream video platforms, became a staple reaction image for Gen Z, and ensured that even those who had never watched a single episode of MyGO knew the penguin [11†L7-L8].

Why We Still Love Her

Tomori the Penguin works because she captures the modern internet‘s favorite form of comedy: a pure, earnest creation corrupted into absolute nonsense while retaining genuine affection. She’s a reminder that sometimes, a shy girl collecting rocks and a screaming penguin from a video game are, somehow, the exact same creature. Whether‘s she’s on a phone case, on a zoo plaque, or screaming into the void — Tomori‘s penguin is here to stay. Gugu-gaga!
