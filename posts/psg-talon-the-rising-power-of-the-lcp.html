PSG Talon: The Rising Power of the LCP

LOL By Lin Published: 2026-06-01 00:00
Founded on December 18, 2017, PSG Talon is a Pacific esports organization established by the video‑sharing platform Bilibili. The organization acquired the I May League of Legends roster to form its LCP division and has since grown into one of the most formidable forces in Pacific competitive League of Legends.

PSG Talon’s journey to the top was gradual but relentless. Early achievements included a runner‑up finish at the 2018 LCP Cup and a fourth‑place finish in the 2019 LCP Summer Split. The breakthrough came in 2022 when PSG Talon claimed the LCP Cup championship. The 2023 season marked PSG Talon’s arrival on the international stage, with runner‑up finishes in both the LCP Spring Split and MSI, alongside a semifinal appearance at the World Championship. In 2024, PSG Talon cemented their domestic dominance by winning both the LCP Spring and Summer Splits. The crowning achievement arrived in March 2026, when PSG Talon defeated GEN.G Esports 3‑1 in the grand final of the First Stand tournament in São Paulo, securing the organization’s first‑ever international League of Legends title.

The current PSG Talon roster is a constellation of elite talent. Top laner Azhi, widely regarded as one of the best in his role, is joined by jungler Junjia, mid laner Maple—a multiple‑time LCP MVP—bot laner Betty, a two‑time First Stand champion, and support Woody. The team is coached by head coach CorGi.

In the 2026 LCP Season, PSG Talon has been utterly dominant. They claimed championships in both the first and second splits. During the second split playoffs, PSG Talon suffered an initial upset loss but then staged a miraculous “one‑through‑five” run—winning five consecutive best‑of‑five series from the lower bracket to claim the title and qualify for MSI as the LCP’s first seed. In a historic feat, all five PSG Talon players were named to the LCP First All‑Pro Team, with Maple winning regular‑season MVP.

With Azhi boldly declaring his intent to defeat CBLOL rivals at MSI, PSG Talon enters the international stage not just as a title contender, but as a team poised to complete the elusive “Golden Road”—an unprecedented sweep of all major titles in a single season.
