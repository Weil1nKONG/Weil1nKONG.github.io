Maodie – The Round-Headed Stray Cat Turned Internet Meme

Internet figures By Weilin Published: 2026-04-18 00:00
In the vast and chaotic world of Chinese internet memes, Maodie – a round-headed orange stray cat – rose to brief but intense fame in 2024 through a series of livestream clips showing his aggressive behavior and refusal to leave a blogger’s home. Unlike polished pet influencers, Maodie became popular precisely because he was uncontrollable, unfriendly, and visually distinctive.

Who Is Maodie?

Maodie was a stray orange tabby with an unusually round head, amber eyes, and a consistently hostile demeanor. He first appeared on the Bilibili channel of a content creator known as ”White Glove & Malinois Wang Cai,” who regularly fed stray cats in her neighborhood. Unlike other strays, Maodie frequently attacked other cats, stole food, scratched the creator hard enough to require rabies shots, and eventually broke into her apartment and refused to leave.

The Viral Moment

Maodie‘s defining viral moment occurred in September 2024 during an incident later nicknamed the ”Battle of the Bookcase.” With doors and windows left open, the cat remained inside for over four hours, hissing repeatedly, arching his back (dubbed ”Spinosaurus Mode” by viewers), and flattening his ears (”airplane ears”). The creator’s livestream footage was clipped and shared millions of times across Bilibili, Douyin, and WeChat. Viewers gave him the nickname ”Round-Headed Maodie” – a pun on a classical Chinese term for the elderly, repurposed to mean ”Father Cat” as a joke about his domineering attitude.

Connection to the Hajimi Meme

Maodie‘s rise coincided with the spread of the ”Hajimi” meme – a nonsensical audio clip originally from the 2021 anime Uma Musume that became widely used as background music for cat videos, especially those featuring aggressive or bizarre feline behavior. Editors began pairing Maodie’s hissing and standoff footage with Hajimi tracks, making him one of the most recognizable faces of the trend. As of 2026, Hajimi edits featuring Maodie remain widely shared.

Physical Characteristics and Catchphrases

Three visual traits became associated with Maodie:

His round head, often singled out in close-up screenshots

”Spinosaurus Mode” – arching his back to appear larger

”Airplane ears” – ears flattened sideways during hissing

A fan-coined catchphrase, ”My big round head is really threatening,” appears frequently in captions and comments alongside his images.

Status as of 2026

Maodie has been reported deceased, but his image and associated clips continue to circulate as reaction memes and Hajimi edits. He is regarded as one of several ”abstract” animal memes from the mid-2020s – notable not for cuteness or heartwarming stories, but for embodying chaotic, uncooperative pet behavior in an ironic online context.
