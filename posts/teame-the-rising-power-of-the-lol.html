TeamE: The Rising Power of the LOL

LOL By Lin Published: 2022-01-01 00:00
TeamE has established itself as the most significant force in its region since first entering the competitive scene in 2020. The organization made history at the 2024 World Championship, where they defeated a strong European opponent 3-2 in an epic grand final to become the first team from their region ever to win an international title. This victory marked a watershed moment not just for the organization, but for the entire region.

The grand final was a rollercoaster affair. Their opponent took the first map 13-6, but TeamE responded with a dominant 13-4 victory on the second map to level the series. TeamE then secured a 2-1 lead with a 13-9 win on the third map, only for their opponent to force a decider with a 13-11 victory on the fourth map. On the final map, TeamE held their nerve, closing out the series to lift the trophy.

TeamE's triumph was powered by extraordinary individual performances. Star player Player1 shattered the world record for most kills in a best-of-five series with 111 total kills across the five maps. Player1 finished atop the leaderboard in three of the five maps and was awarded the first-ever MVP trophy for the World Championship. Teammate Player2 also delivered a masterclass on the third map with 27 kills, while rookie Player3 completed the "royal road" — winning the championship in their debut year after playing in the developmental league just three months prior.

The championship lineup featured Player1, Player2, Player3, Player4, and Player5, coached by Coach1. Since that historic victory, TeamE has continued to dominate domestically. In May 2026, they defeated a domestic opponent 3-2 in the regional Stage 1 finals to claim their ninth regional title — a record that underscores their sustained excellence. The core quartet of Player1, Player2, Player4, and Player5 have been present for all nine championships.

At the 2026 international mid-season tournament, TeamE entered as their region's top seed. They secured a top-three finish by defeating a domestic rival 2-1 in the upper bracket semifinal, with a dominant 13-3 victory on the decider map. Their starting roster for the tournament featured Player1, Player2, Player4, Player5, and Player6. TeamE's journey from their early international appearances — a 32nd-place finish at a 2023 international invitational, followed by top-six finishes at two major global events later that year — to world champions in 2024 reflects a steady ascent built on resilience and growth.

Today, TeamE remains the benchmark for excellence in their region — the first team to break through on the global stage and a perennial contender who continues to push for more. The organization has invested heavily in training infrastructure, scouting networks, and coaching development, ensuring a pipeline of talent that sustains their competitive edge across multiple seasons and tournament cycles.
