LYON: The Rising Power of the LCS

LOL By Lin Published: 2026-05-01 00:00
Founded on April 2, 2013, LYON is a Mexican esports organization established by Karzek, FraGio, and Nikez. The organization has since grown into one of the most formidable forces in North American competitive League of Legends.

LYON’s journey to the top was gradual but relentless. Early achievements included a championship at the 2018 LLN Open Cup and a first-place finish in the 2020 LLA Summer Split. The breakthrough came in 2024 when LYON claimed the LLA Closing Split championship. The 2025 season marked LYON’s transition to the newly merged LTA, and in March 2026, LYON cemented their domestic dominance by winning the LCS 2026 Spring Split championship. The crowning achievement arrived shortly after, when LYON defeated GEN.G Esports 3‑1 in the grand final of the First Stand tournament in São Paulo, securing the organization’s first‑ever international League of Legends title.

The current LYON roster is a constellation of elite talent. Top laner Niship “Dhokla” Doshi, widely regarded as one of the best in his role, is joined by jungler Kacper “Inspired” Słoma, mid laner Kang “Saint” Seong‑in—a multiple‑time LCS MVP—bot laner Kim “Berserker” Min‑cheol, a two‑time First Stand champion, and support Jonah “Isles” Rosario. The team is coached by head coach Kim “Reignover” Yeu‑jin.

In the 2026 LCS Season, LYON has been utterly dominant. They claimed championships in both the first and second splits. During the second split playoffs, LYON suffered an initial upset loss but then staged a miraculous “one‑through‑five” run—winning five consecutive best‑of‑five series from the lower bracket to claim the title and qualify for MSI as the LCS’s first seed. In a historic feat, all five LYON players were named to the LCS First All‑Pro Team, with Saint winning regular‑season MVP.

With Dhokla boldly declaring his intent to defeat CBLOL rivals at MSI, LYON enters the international stage not just as a title contender, but as a team poised to complete the elusive “Golden Road”—an unprecedented sweep of all major titles in a single season.
