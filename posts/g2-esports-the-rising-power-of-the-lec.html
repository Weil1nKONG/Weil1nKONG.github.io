G2 Esports: The Rising Power of the LEC

LOL By Lin Published: 2026-03-01 00:00
Founded on December 18, 2017, G2 Esports is a European esports organization established by the video‑sharing platform Bilibili. The organization acquired the I May League of Legends roster to form its LEC division and has since grown into one of the most formidable forces in European competitive League of Legends.

G2’s journey to the top was gradual but relentless. Early achievements included a runner‑up finish at the 2018 LEC Cup and a fourth‑place finish in the 2019 LEC Summer Split. The breakthrough came in 2022 when G2 claimed the LEC Cup championship. The 2023 season marked G2’s arrival on the international stage, with runner‑up finishes in both the LEC Spring Split and MSI, alongside a semifinal appearance at the World Championship. In 2024, G2 cemented their domestic dominance by winning both the LEC Spring and Summer Splits. The crowning achievement arrived in March 2026, when G2 defeated T1 3‑1 in the grand final of the First Stand tournament in São Paulo, securing the organization’s first‑ever international League of Legends title.

The current G2 roster is a constellation of elite talent. Top laner Sergen “BrokenBlade” Çelik, widely regarded as one of the best in his role, is joined by jungler Martin “Yike” Sundelin, mid laner Rasmus “Caps” Winther—a multiple‑time LEC MVP—bot laner Steven “Hans Sama” Liv, a two‑time First Stand champion, and support Mihael “Mikyx” Mehle. The team is coached by head coach Dylan Falco.

In the 2026 LEC Season, G2 has been utterly dominant. They claimed championships in both the first and second splits. During the second split playoffs, G2 suffered an initial upset loss but then staged a miraculous “one‑through‑five” run—winning five consecutive best‑of‑five series from the lower bracket to claim the title and qualify for MSI as the LEC’s first seed. In a historic feat, all five G2 players were named to the LEC First All‑Pro Team, with Caps winning regular‑season MVP.

With BrokenBlade boldly declaring his intent to defeat LCK rivals at MSI, G2 enters the international stage not just as a title contender, but as a team poised to complete the elusive “Golden Road”—an unprecedented sweep of all major titles in a single season.
