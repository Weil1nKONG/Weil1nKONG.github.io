Manbo – The Pink-Haired Horse Girl Who Became Abstract Royalty

Internet figures By Weilin Published: 2026-04-18 00:00
In the strange, self-perpetuating universe of Chinese internet memes, few faces have achieved the hypnotic ubiquity of Manbo – a pink-haired, blank-eyed anime girl whose looping chant of “Manbo… manbo… oh matsuri manbo…” has colonized short-video platforms, spawned thousands of AI covers, and become virtually inseparable from its sister meme Hajimi. Debuting originally as a minor character in a Japanese mobile game, Manbo has since escaped her source material entirely, becoming a blank canvas for anonymous storytellers, music remixers, and abstract humorists. As of 2026, she is one of the internet‘s most recognizable “faceless” icons – often recognized by people who have no idea who she actually is.

Who Is Manbo?

Manbo’s face belongs to Shikyūgeki (also known as “Poetry Drama”), a supporting character from the Japanese mobile game Uma Musume: Pretty Derby, released in 2021. Shikyūgeki is a short, pink-haired “horse girl” based on a real-life racehorse named Machikane Tannhuser, who famously missed a major race due to a nosebleed – a quirk that carried over into her in-game persona as a clumsy, dim-witted character frequently seen dripping blood from her nostrils. In her personal story, she cheerfully recites a nonsensical motto taught by her father: “Manbo… manbo… oh matsuri manbo…” – a jingle whose only literal meaning is the Japanese word for “ocean sunfish,” a fish whose swimming motion resembles a mambo dance. Within the game‘s niche fandom, the phrase was a cute but forgettable detail.

The Viral Takeoff

Manbo’s escape from obscurity began on Bilibili around late 2023, when a creator named “Yigen Huazi” began producing short AI-dubbed skits and absurdist music remixes featuring Shikyūgeki‘s chibi (super-deformed) sprite – her oversized head, vacant eyes, and the looping “Manbo” chant. The effect was instant. Viewers who had never touched Uma Musume shared the clips anyway, drawn by the audio’s strange, trance-like rhythm. The comments filled with demands for longer versions, weirder edits, and crossovers with other memes. By mid-2024, “Manbo” had detached entirely from its original character, becoming its own meme format.

The Manbo Multiverse

What followed was an explosion of derivative content that users dubbed the ”Manbo Multiverse.” The core format is simple: take any existing song, replace the vocals with an AI voice trained on Shikyūgeki‘s cadence (or simply pitch-shifted nonsense), and let the “Manbo” chorus do the rest. By 2025, AI music tools had lowered the barrier so much that anyone could generate a Manbo cover in under an hour. Alongside music edits, a second genre emerged: Manbo storytelling videos. These clips feature Shikyūgeki’s chibi face cycling through low-effort stock images while a synthesized voice narrates outrageous first-person anecdotes – workplace betrayals, school humiliations, viral drama – always beginning with the deadpan phrase ”Bùguǎnle” (“Screw it”). Thousands of anonymous Douyin and Bilibili accounts now produce nearly identical Manbo narration videos, with viewers often unable to tell one channel from another.

Collision with Hajimi

Manbo‘s rise ran parallel to that of Hajimi – a similarly nonsensical chant performed by a different Uma Musume character, Tokai Teio. As both audio loops spread, ordinary users conflated the two, pairing Hajimi audio with Shikyūgeki’s face and Manbo audio with unrelated visuals. By mid-2025, the two memes were functionally inseparable. To most netizens, “Hajimi” was that pink-haired horse girl who said “Manbo,” and “Manbo” was that cat meme about Japanese honey. The original sources no longer mattered.

Legacy – A Meme as a Platform

Manbo represents a new form of viral content: characterless yet inseparable from a character, anonymous yet visually fixed, and infinitely malleable. In 2026, a fan-made ”Manbo Spring Festival Gala” compiled contributions from over 200 independent creators, including music parodies, skits, and crossovers with unrelated memes like the orange cat “Maodie.” The original game‘s copyright holders have never publicly acknowledged the phenomenon, and Bilibili’s algorithm continues to serve her face to users who have never heard of Uma Musume. Manbo has become the internet’s most beautiful piece of nonsense – meaningless, repurposed, impossible to escape. Scroll through any short-video feed. Wait ten seconds. You’ll hear it. Manbo… manbo… oh mah-tsu-ri manbo…
