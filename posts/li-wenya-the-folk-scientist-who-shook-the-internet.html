Li Wenya – The Folk Scientist Who Shook the Internet

Internet figures By Weilin Published: 2026-04-18 00:00
In the bizarre and endlessly entertaining corners of China‘s short-video platforms, few figures have sparked as much bewilderment, laughter, and genuine fascination as Li Wenya, better known to his followers as simply ”Professor Li.“ Debuting on Douyin (China’s TikTok) in late 2024, this retired senior citizen has since become one of the most iconic and tragicomic ”folk science“ (mínkē) phenomena of the internet age.

Who Is Professor Li?

Li Wenya is no ordinary elderly content creator — he is a self-taught, fiercely passionate, and utterly convinced revolutionary physicist who claims to have single-handedly overthrown the entire foundation of modern science. Born in January 1954 in Dengzhou, Henan Province, Li holds a high school education — respectable for his generation, but a far cry from the academic pedigree of the titans he seeks to dethrone. In 1999, he moved to Shenzhen and worked as a university cleaner and gardener before running a flower shop. By his own account, the turning point came around 2000 when he found a children‘s book titled Unsolved Mysteries of the Universe near his shop’s entrance. Inspired, he sold his business, returned to his hometown, and sequestered himself for over a decade to “research” physics.

The Wenya Four Laws — A New Physics?

Li Wenya‘s theoretical framework is famously encapsulated in the ”Wenya Four Laws,” a set of propositions that directly challenge the bedrock of classical and modern physics:

First Law: ”Energy is possessed only by moving objects; stationary objects have zero energy.“

Second Law: ”Energy can be created and destroyed, never transferred from one object to another“ — effectively rejecting the law of conservation of energy.

Third Law: Time is material.

Fourth Law: Energy is always kinetic and equals mass times velocity (E=mv), a direct rejection of Einstein‘s E=mc².

From these core principles, Li has developed his most famous proposition: the ”Earth Tumbling Motion Theory,” which posits that Earth does not rotate or revolve around the Sun as scientists claim. Instead, he argues, Earth undergoes a complex motion consisting of twelve distinct movements, including tumbling, swinging, reversing, ascending, descending, and orbiting in a trapezoidal path. Li claims to have observed these motions by using a basketball to simulate celestial bodies and observing the Sun with his naked eye — a methodology he calls ”grounded science“.

The War Against Sun Xiaochuan

What truly launched Li Wenya from obscure folk scientist to internet legend was not his physics, however, but a spectacular misunderstanding of online trolling. In mid-2025, a Douyin user jokingly claimed that a fictional ”Professor Sun Xiaochuan of Wuhan University“ had publicly denounced Li‘s theories. ”Sun Xiaochuan“ is in reality an internet meme figure known for being the target of absurd satirical accusations, but Li, completely unfamiliar with internet culture, took the comment literally.

Enraged, Li declared war on this imagined academic rival, accusing ”Professor Sun“ of stealing his Nobel Prize–worthy research with a USB drive, leading a global conspiracy called the 114514 Research Institute to suppress his work, and even using ”brain control devices“ to sabotage his computer. He filmed dozens of increasingly frantic video rebuttals, demanded live debates, and announced that he was permanently rejecting the Nobel Prize in protest.

The Quiet End

Li‘s main Douyin account was reportedly terminated around June 10, 2026, likely due to repeated platform violations. His final months online were marked by increasingly surreal content, including wearing animal headgear while denouncing Einstein and monologues about his computers being hacked.

Li Wenya’s story is less about science than about human frailty in the digital age — a lonely, stubborn, and genuinely convinced man who became the internet‘s most tragicomic ”professor.“ Whether he is a delusional sufferer, a lonely old man starved for attention, or the most committed performance artist remains debated. But one thing is certain: Li Wenya has left the building — probably to file another complaint about his WiFi.
