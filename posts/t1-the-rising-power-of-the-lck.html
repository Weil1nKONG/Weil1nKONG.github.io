T1: The Rising Power of the LCK

LOL By Lin Published: 2026-02-01 00:00
Founded on December 18, 2017, T1 is a Korean esports organization established by the renowned telecommunications company SK Telecom. The organization acquired the I May League of Legends roster to form its LCK division and has since grown into one of the most formidable forces in Korean competitive League of Legends.

T1’s journey to the top was gradual but relentless. Early achievements included a runner-up finish at the 2018 KeSPA Cup and a fourth‑place finish in the 2019 LCK Summer Split. The breakthrough came in 2022 when T1 claimed the KeSPA Cup championship. The 2023 season marked T1’s arrival on the international stage, with runner-up finishes in both the LCK Spring Split and MSI, alongside a semifinal appearance at the World Championship. In 2024, T1 cemented their domestic dominance by winning both the LCK Spring and Summer Splits. The crowning achievement arrived in March 2026, when T1 defeated GEN.G Esports 3‑1 in the grand final of the First Stand tournament in São Paulo, securing the organization’s first‑ever international League of Legends title.

The current T1 roster is a constellation of elite talent. Top laner Choi “Zeus” Woo‑je, widely regarded as one of the best in his role, is joined by jungler Mun “Oner” Hyeon‑jun, mid laner Lee “Faker” Sang‑hyeok—a multiple‑time LCK MVP —bot laner Lee “Gumayusi” Min‑hyung, a two‑time First Stand champion, and support Ryu “Keria” Min‑seok. The team is coached by head coach Kim “kkOma” Jeong‑gyun.

In the 2026 LCK Season, T1 has been utterly dominant. They claimed championships in both the first and second splits. During the second split playoffs, T1 suffered an initial upset loss but then staged a miraculous “one‑through‑five” run—winning five consecutive best‑of‑five series from the lower bracket to claim the title and qualify for MSI as the LCK’s first seed. In a historic feat, all five T1 players were named to the LCK First All‑Pro Team, with Faker winning regular‑season MVP.

With Zeus boldly declaring his intent to defeat CBLOL rivals at MSI, T1 enters the international stage not just as a title contender, but as a team poised to complete the elusive “Golden Road”—an unprecedented sweep of all major titles in a single season.
