Nai Long – The Unstoppable Yellow Dragon Conquering the World

Internet figures By Weilin Published: 2026-04-18 00:00
In the vast and ever-crowded universe of Chinese animation IPs, few characters have risen as meteorically — or as controversially — as Nai Long, better known to his millions of fans as simply ”Milk Dragon.” Debuting on January 22, 2020, this chubby, golden-yellow dinosaur from another planet has since amassed a staggering 39 million followers across global platforms as of 2026, becoming one of China‘s most recognizable and polarizing animated sensations.

Who Is Nai Long?

Nai Long is no ordinary cartoon character — he is an adorable, dim-witted, and perpetually hungry baby dragon from a distant star who crash-lands on Earth. There, he befriends a genius Chinese boy named Xiao Qi, and together they embark on hilarious, bite-sized adventures across mystical dimensions using Xiao Qi’s inventions.

Born with a ”Duang~Duang“ belly, a 3.5-year-old mentality, and weighing a whopping 2 tons despite standing only 180 cm tall, Nai Long is a walking contradiction. His yellow skin and round, expressionless eyes have become instantly recognizable memes across WeChat, TikTok, and beyond. His signature line? ”My big tummy is really cool, cool!” — a mantra he lives by as he dreams of becoming the dragon with the ”biggest head in the village,” despite his unwavering commitment to eating everything in sight.

From Shorts to Superstardom

The brainchild of creator Xie Cheng’en (affectionately called ”Dragon Daddy”), Nai Long was born from a childhood fascination with dinosaurs and a desire to bring simple, healing joy to stressed-out modern audiences. The IP exploded on Douyin (China’s TikTok), where short 60-second episodes featuring Nai Long‘s clumsy antics racked up 2.2 billion likes and 419 videos as of March 2026.

Since then, Nai Long has expanded into a multimedia empire: 246 animated episodes across Youku, iQiyi, and Tencent Video, a theme song ”Dudu,” over 200 brand collaborations (including McDonald‘s, Miniso, and Pop Mart), and even a 20-meter inflatable version appearing at Shenzhen parks. In 2025, the series Nai Long’s Wonderful Adventures won a Golden Angel Award at the Sino-American TV Festival and was nominated for Best Preschool Program at the Asian TV Awards.

The Dragon That Breaks the Internet — For Better or Worse

Nai Long‘s success is anything but simple. While originally aimed at children under 10, his animations are packed with recycled internet slang, rapid-fire cuts, and goofy dances — leading some parents and critics to label him a ”toxic animation” that harms attention spans.

Even more striking is his unlikely fame among adults — but not always as a fan. Nai Long became a target of ”abstract culture” (chōuxiàng), where young netizens remix his image into bizarre, sometimes violent parodies: ”Ultraman beating Nai Long” videos, the ”Tang Long” insult (implying mental slowness), and memes labeling him ”cringe”.

Despite the backlash — or perhaps because of it — Nai Long has leaned into the chaos. The official brand now embraces the memes, even inviting critics to collaborate. This fearless self-deprecation has turned haters into reluctant promoters, fueling his rise from kiddie content to a bizarre pop-culture phenomenon.

Global Ambitions — From Shenzhen to the World

Never one to stay local, Nai Long has gone global in spectacular fashion. In mid-2025, a single TikTok video asking ”Why does my girlfriend only reply with Nai Long stickers?” racked up 2.3 million likes, sparking a worldwide meme frenzy. Today, Nai Long‘s stickers have been downloaded over 500,000 times on WhatsApp, his YouTube episodes pull nearly 2 million views per episode, and he’s been featured on Instagram posts by Canadian singer Tate McRae, who put him on her phone case.

With an overseas presence spanning 57 countries and 15 languages, Nai Long has been spotted at the Annecy International Animation Festival and in Southeast Asia‘s hit game Free Fire. Whether you love him or cringe at him, one thing is certain: Nai Long is impossible to ignore — and impossible to escape. My big tummy is really cool, cool!
